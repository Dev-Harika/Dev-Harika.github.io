I grew up curious about how systems work, not just the software, but the invisible scaffolding that keeps everything running at scale. That curiosity took me from Hyderabad to Missouri to Texas, chasing problems that actually matter.

My career started in infrastructure, migrating 38 legacy applications to AWS at NJ Transit and learning early that the hardest part of any migration isn't the technology. It's making the transition invisible to the thousands of people who depend on it every day.

From there I moved into multi-cloud engineering at Dun & Bradstreet, leading a zero-downtime migration from AWS to GCP while keeping production services untouched. That's when I realized I was most energized by the problems that scare other engineers: live production cutovers, regulated environments, systems with no room for error.

Today I build the infrastructure that runs AI. At Cloud2Labs, I deploy large language models like Llama 3, Mistral, and DeepSeek across Kubernetes clusters and GPU accelerators for Dell Technologies and Intel. I'm also a contributor to Intel's open-source OPEA project, where my work on bare-metal deployments has been featured in Dell Technologies InfoHub and adopted by enterprise teams worldwide.

What I care about: infrastructure reliable enough to be boring, automation that gives engineers their time back, and platforms that let AI researchers focus on ideas instead of ops.
